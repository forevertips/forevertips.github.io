First, you need to open the game or application you want to take a screenshot of. Once it has loaded up, you will need to open the Steam Deck overlay. To do this, press “Shift+Tab” on your keyboard. This will open up a small overlay which gives you access to some of Steam’s features.

Now that the overlay has opened, you will need to click on the “Screenshot” section of the overlay. This will open up a menu with three different options. The first option is the “Take Screenshot” button which will let you capture the image of the game or application you have open. The second option is the “Screenshot Library” where you can view, edit, and share all the screenshots you have taken. Finally, click on the “Settings” option to customize the settings for taking screenshots.

At this point, you’re ready to take your first screenshot. Click on the “Take Screenshot” option and a snapshot of your game or application will be taken and stored in your Steam Library. To share or save your screenshot, just click on the “Share” or “Save” options located in the screenshot Library.

2. Capturing and Saving Steam Deck Screenshots#

Steam Deck is a gaming platform allowing users to access a huge library of games and save their in-game progress. It also gives an opportunity to take screenshots of each game in order to remember the moment or share it with friends. Taking screenshots on Steam Deck is surprisingly easy and only requires a few clicks.

First, launch the desired game. Then, press F12 on the keyboard that will capture your current screen. Your screenshots will be saved into Pictures folder inside the Steam directory.

It’s also possible to take screenshots of desktop, or even any other game outside of Steam. To do that, open the Steam Overlay (default is Shift+Tab) and press F12 there. All screenshots taken outside of a Steam game will show up in the Screenshots folder in the Steam directory.

If you want to adjust the settings, go to the Steam settings page and click on the In-game tab. There you can enable the Steam overlay, change the default screenshot key and set the maximum resolution.
