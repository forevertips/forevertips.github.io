2. Transferring the Voice Memo#

Voice memos provide a convenient way to capture ideas and reminders quickly, making them great for recording lectures, brainstorming sessions, and more. It is easy to record a voice memo on an Android device, but it can be a bit tricky to transfer those recordings to another device. Here are the steps to transfer your voice memo recordings from your Android device.

First, you need to open your device’s file manager. Then, head to the “My Files” folder and select “Sounds and Vibrations.” Within this folder, you should find all of your voice memo recordings.

To transfer the recordings, you can either copy them to a USB flash drive or send them through Bluetooth. To copy them to a USB drive, simply select the recordings you want to transfer, and then drag them into the USB drive. To send them through Bluetooth, select the files you want to transfer and then click “Share.” Select “Bluetooth,” and then pair your device with the device you want to send the recordings to.

Once the recordings have been sent, you can delete them from your device if you want to free up storage space. To do this, select the recordings you want to delete and then click “Delete.” Confirm that you want to delete the recordings, and then they will be permanently removed from your device.

By following these steps, you can easily and quickly transfer your voice memo recordings from your Android device to another device.
