To take a screenshot on a Microsoft laptop, press the Windows logo key + PrtScn. That will save a full-screen screenshot to your laptop’s Pictures folder.

Q2. How do I take a screenshot of just one window on my Microsoft laptop? A2. To take a screenshot of just one window on your Microsoft laptop, press Alt + PrtScn. That will take a picture of just the active window and copy it to the clipboard. You can then paste the image into an image editor or other program.

Q3. How do I take a delayed screenshot on my Microsoft laptop? A3. To take a delayed screenshot on your Microsoft laptop, press the Windows logo key + Shift + S. That will open the snipping bar. From there, you can choose a type of snip and then click and drag your mouse to capture the area of the screen you want.

Q4. Where can I find the screenshots I’ve taken on my Microsoft laptop? A4. All screenshots you take on your Microsoft laptop will automatically be saved in the Pictures folder. You can find this by opening the File Explorer and clicking on “Pictures.”

Q5. What is the best image format for me to save my screenshots in on my Microsoft laptop? A5. The best image format to save your screenshots in on your Microsoft laptop is PNG. That format is a lossless compression file format, meaning that any image quality you’ve captured is retained and won’t be lost after the screenshot has been saved.
