Q1. What is a screenshot?

A1. A screenshot is a digital image of what appears on the display of a computer, smartphone, or other device. It can be used for various purposes such as troubleshooting, document sharing, and creating tutorials.

Q2. What are the different ways to take a screenshot on the iPad Pro 2022?

A2. The iPad Pro 2022 offers several ways to take a screenshot. You can use the Sleep/Wake + Home button combo, the Assistive Touch button, or the “Duplicate” option in the Share Sheet.

Q3. How do I use the Sleep/Wake + Home button combo to take a screenshot on the iPad Pro 2022?

A3. To take a screenshot using the Sleep/Wake + Home combo, press and hold the sleep/wake button located at the top right of the iPad Pro, along with the Home button at the same time for a few seconds. You should hear a shutter sound confirm the screenshot was taken and it will be saved in your photo library for you to edit and share.

Q4. How do I use the Assistive Touch button to take a screenshot on the iPad Pro 2022?

A4. To take a screenshot using the Assistive Touch button, enable Assistive Touch by going to Settings > Accessibility > Assistive Touch and turning it on. Then, tap the Assistive Touch button and choose the ‘Device’ option. Select the ‘Screenshot’ option and your screenshot will be saved to your device’s photos.

Q5. What is the “Duplicate” option in the Share Sheet?

A5. The “Duplicate” option in the Share Sheet allows you to duplicate whatever is on your display and save it as a file, without taking a screenshot. This is particularly useful for copying text, charts, and images. To use this feature, open the Share Sheet, located by swiping up from the bottom of the screen, and select ‘Duplicate’. The file will be saved in your ‘Files’ app.
