1. What is Screenshot and How to Do it on Mac#

A screenshot is a digital image of what’s displayed on your computer screen. It’s a handy tool for sharing what’s on your Mac’s display with others, and a great way to capture and keep information that you don’t want to lose. But sometimes, it’s difficult to capture a long webpage or document, and that’s where scrolling screenshots come in. This article shows you how to easily take scrolling screenshots on your Mac.

The first step is to open the window you want to capture with the entire content visible onscreen. Press Command-Shift-4 on your keyboard to turn your cursor into a crosshair. Press and hold the Spacebar and you’ll see the crosshair turn into a little camera icon. Move the camera to the top of the window that you want to capture, then click your mouse button or trackpad and drag downward. You’ll see a translucent blue highlight appear around the window. When you release the mouse button or trackpad, the screenshot will be automatically saved to your desktop.

Next, open the screenshot in Preview, and click on “View” > “Scroll View” at the top of the window. All you need to do is to click and hold the window with the left mouse button, and drag downwards to capture the whole document. Once finished, you can save the file in any image format you want.

To quickly capture a scrolling screenshot from a webpage, you can use the built-in Safari command. First, open Safari and navigate to the page that you want to capture. Then, select “View” > “Take a Screenshot” and select the “Entire Page” option. The scrolling screenshot will be automatically saved to your Downloads folder.

By following these steps, you can easily take scrolling screenshots on your Mac in no time. With the help of this article, you can capture and share long webpages or documents with ease.
