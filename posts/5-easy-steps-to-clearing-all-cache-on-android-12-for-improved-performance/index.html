Are you experiencing a slower performance on your Android 12? Don’t worry, you can easily restore your device’s performance by clearing all cache on your Android 12 with these 5 easy steps. Find out how to do it right here! Cleaning your Android device’s cache may seem like a bit of a hassle, but it can make a big difference in improving your device’s performance. Having too much cache on your Android device can cause it to slow down and even become unresponsive at times. Fortunately, it’s surprisingly easy to clear the cache on an Android device. Here are the five easy steps to clearing all cache on your Android 12 device and getting back to optimum performance.

Step 1: Open the Settings app. Open your Settings app, located in the App Drawer. Swipe left or right until you see the “Device” tab, and tap on it.

Step 2: Select Storage. From the Device tab, select Storage, and then select “Storage” again. You’ll see a list of all of your apps and the amount of storage they’re taking up.

Step 3: Select the App(s) You Want to Clear the Cache From. Scroll down the list until you find the app you want to clear the cache from. Once you select the app, you’ll see an option for “Clear Cache”.

Step 4: Confirm the Cache Clearing. Once you select the “Clear Cache” option, a pop-up window will appear asking if you’re sure you want to delete the cache from the app. Select the “OK” button to confirm the cache clearing.

Step 5: Restart Your Device. After clearing the cache from all of the apps you selected, you’ll need to restart your device in order for the changes to take effect. To do this, press and hold the power button until you see the “restart” option. Select the restart button, and your device will reboot.

Now that you’ve cleared all of the cache from your Android 12 device, it should be running a lot smoother and faster. Keep it running at its peak performance by regularly cleaning out its cache. .
